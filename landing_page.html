Our Journey Through Time

1950s - The Foundation

1954-55

A Diploma Program (DIISc) was started, designed solely for the executives of Public sector.

1957

The Section of Industrial Engineering and Administration was set up.

This represents the earliest attempt at an academic program in management for India's industrial enterprises by systematically training manpower and generating knowledge.

1960s & 1970s - Expansion

1960

A research program was introduced known as Master of IISc.

1965

The Section of Economics and Social Sciences and the Industrial Engineering & Administration were merged to form "Department of Industrial Management".

1967

Introduction of one year PG Diploma program.

1970s

The Department's research was broadened to include management problems of:

  • R&D organizations
  • Sophisticated science-based industries
  • Development of science policy in the country
1980s - Transformation

1982-1985

Focus on national issues including:

  • Public Sector Enterprises
  • Economic Planning
  • Small Scale Industries

1985

Industrial Management was renamed as Department of Management Studies.

One year Diploma Program replaced by one and a half year M.Tech Program in Management Studies.

1990s - Diversification

1990s

The Department research focus was broadened to include:

  • Energy planning with bottom-up approach
  • Integration of technologies of energy supply and energy efficiency
  • Internalizing environmental concerns
  • Rural technologies

1990

The Operations Research Society of India Bangalore Chapter was set up and a national conference was conducted.

Research program diversified by including problem solving approaches employing OR methods.

1997

National Doctoral Consortium in Management Research for students was formed.

National level research conference for management research students was conducted in 1997 & 1998.

2000s - Globalization

2000s

  • MBA program introduced
  • Integration of Foreign Languages section
  • Re-introduction of National Doctoral Consortium as Consortium of Students in Management Research (COSMAR) in 2001
  • COSMAR Conference became an annual event

2000s

Key events and collaborations:

  • NIPM Conference
  • Cyber Entrepreneurship Conference
  • Expert Panel Discussions on the Union Budget
  • IISc-Max Planck Institute of Economics Conference
  • IISc-University of Minnesota: Technology Management Workshop
  • International Collaboration with George Mason University and Pforzheim Business School
2010s and Beyond - Innovation

2010s

  • M.Mgt program replaces the MBA program
  • Specialization in Business Analytics and Technology Management

Present

Having completed our Diamond Jubilee Year, we are now marching towards our Platinum Jubilee with renewed vigor and commitment to excellence in management education and research.
